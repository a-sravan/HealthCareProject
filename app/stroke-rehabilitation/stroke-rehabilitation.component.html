STROKE REHABILITATION

A stroke occurs when the blood flow to a specific part of your brain is cut off. When this happens, the cells don’t get oxygen and begin to die, causing numerous symptoms. The most common symptoms are changes in speech and numbness or weakness of the face, legs, or arms.

The quick assessment for stroke, known by the acronym FAST (face, arms, speech, time), is seen below:

F

Check for facial droop.

A

Hold arms out. Does one drop downward?

S

Is speech abnormal, delayed, or absent?

T

It’s time to call local emergency service if any of these symptoms are present.

Strokes can cause significant impairment in language, cognition, motor, and sensory skills. This is why it’s considered to be a leading cause of serious long-term disability.

Successful stroke recovery depends on a number of factors, including:

How much damage the stroke caused
How soon recovery starts
Inner motivation & outlook towards recovery
Age, when it happened
Existing comorbidities, that can affect recovery

At Heka our experienced team of Doctors along with the dedicated care manager offer rehabilitation care and helps to meet your stroke recovery goals, who plan and coordinate rehab program to help meet your stroke recovery goals.

1. Physiatrist – specialises in rehabilitation following injuries, accidents or illness.

2. Neurologist – specialises in the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of stroke and other diseases of the brain and spinal cord.

3. Rehabilitation nurse – helps people with disabilities and helps survivors manage health problems like diabetes and high blood pressure and adjust to life after stroke.

4. Physical therapist – helps with problems in moving and balance, suggesting exercises to strengthen muscles for walking, standing and other activities.

5. Occupational therapist – helps with strategies to manage daily activities such as eating, bathing, dressing, writing and cooking.

6. Speech-therapist – helps with talking, reading and writing, and shares strategies to help with swallowing problems.

7. Dietician – healthy eating and special diets low in sodium, fat and calories.

8. Neuropsychologist – diagnoses and treats survivors who face changes in thinking, memory and behaviour.

9. Recreation Therapist. Helps with strategies to improve the thinking and movement skills needed to join in recreational activities.
