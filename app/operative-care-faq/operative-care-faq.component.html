Frequently Asked Questions

Postoperative care is the care and treatment provided to a patient following surgery. This frequently includes wound care and pain management following surgery. Naturally, the type of care you will receive is determined by the type of surgery you had, your medical history, and any complications that occurred following the surgery.

  • Wound care and dressing
  • Using pain relievers to prevent infection, alleviate symptoms, and manage pain
  • Keeping feeding tubes, intravenous (IV) lines, and drains in place
  • Taking blood or other fluid samples
  • Managing a Postoperative Complication

Careless mistakes can be costly when your body is still recovering. In a less severe scenario, pushing yourself too soon after surgery can impede your recovery. At worst, it could reverse your surgical progress or result in a medical emergency.

Pay attention to your doctor's postoperative care recommendations if you want to be fully healed as soon as possible and avoid additional invasive procedures.
